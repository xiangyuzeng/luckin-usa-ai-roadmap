5 Key Findings
  • US00004 "37th & Broadway" is the #1 cannibalization suspect: opened Nov 2025 on the same Broadway corridor, 2.5 km distance. Its revenue growth from $0 to $55.8K/month mirrors 8th Ave's decline from $106K to $51.8K over the same period.
  • Revenue transferred: estimated $25K–$40K/month migrated from 8th Ave to 37th & Broadway. The combined revenue of both stores (~$107K in Jan) closely matches 8th Ave's Oct peak ($106K), suggesting near-zero net new demand generation.
  • US00008 "33rd & 10th" is a secondary suspect: opened Dec 2025, 2.7 km distance, on the west side. Its opening correlates with the acceleration of 8th Ave's decline (from -19% MoM to -24% MoM).
  • US00002 "28th & 6th" (closest at 1.7 km) shows stable revenue (~$74K–$82K) — opened same day as 8th Ave in Jun 2025 and serves a distinct customer base (office workers in NoMad vs. commuter foot traffic on Broadway). This indicates proximity alone is insufficient to predict cannibalization.
  • Corridor effect matters more than distance: stores on the same arterial route (Broadway) compete for the same pedestrian foot traffic flow, even at 2.5 km separation. Cross-avenue stores (28th & 6th vs. 8th & Broadway) capture different flow patterns.
6 Recommendations
  1. Mandatory cannibalization impact modeling before all future store openings. Model should account for corridor alignment, pedestrian flow patterns, and time-of-day demand overlap — not just geographic radius.
  2. Minimum recommended spacing: 2.5 km for same-corridor stores. For cross-avenue locations, the minimum can be reduced to 1.5 km, as demonstrated by the stable coexistence of 8th & Broadway and 28th & 6th.
  3. Implement post-opening monitoring: automatically track the 3 nearest stores' daily revenue for 90 days after each new store opening. Trigger alerts if any nearby store shows >10% MoM decline within 60 days of a new opening.
  4. Consider differentiated offerings for stores in close proximity: distinct menu emphasis (e.g., morning pastry focus vs. afternoon beverage focus), loyalty program incentives to retain customers at the original location, or staggered operating hours.
  5. Immediate action for 8th & Broadway: evaluate promotional campaigns, loyalty-based retention offers, and localized menu differentiation to recover lost customer traffic. Monitor whether stabilization occurs naturally as 37th & Broadway matures and saturates.
