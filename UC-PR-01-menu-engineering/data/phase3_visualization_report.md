## 2. Methodology

### Classification Thresholds
- **Popularity threshold**: 1/N × 70% = 1/58 × 70 = **1.21%** (Kasavana-Smith standard)
- **CM% threshold**: Revenue-weighted avg = total CM$ / total revenue × 100 = **56.8%**
  - Formula: Σ(total_cm) / Σ(total_revenue) × 100
  - Input: $1,057,114 total CM / $1,860,550 total revenue

### Scatter Plot Encoding
- **X-axis**: Sales Mix % (popularity)
- **Y-axis**: Contribution Margin % (profitability)
- **Bubble size**: Proportional to total CM$ (larger = more total margin contribution)
- **Color**: Star (green), Plow Horse (blue), Puzzle (orange), Dog (red)
- **Background shading**: Light quadrant coloring for visual separation

## 3. Key Visual Insights

### Chart 01 — Main BCG Matrix
- **Drip Coffee** stands alone in the upper-right: highest CM% (82.1%) at 7.29% sales mix — the most efficient Star
- **Iced Coconut Latte** dominates by volume (12.5% sales mix) but has moderate CM% (61.3%) — volume-driven Star
- Clear separation between high-margin Puzzles (top-left cluster) and low-margin Dogs (bottom-left)
- **Pistachio Oat Latte (Hot)** is a CM outlier at -2.6% — the only negative-margin product

### Chart 02 — Category Breakdown
- **Hot beverages**: 16 items, dominated by Stars (Drip Coffee, Latte, Cappuccino)
- **Iced beverages**: 33 items, most diverse spread across all 4 quadrants
- **Food items**: 5 items only, clustered as Puzzles (low volume, decent margin)
- Summary table confirms food is under-represented in the menu

### Chart 03 — Margin vs Discount
- Strong negative correlation: deeper discounts → lower CM%
- Products at ~50% discount depth tend to cluster at 40-60% CM%
- Low-discount items (food at ~13%) achieve 55-65% CM% despite lower list prices
- Suggests **discount control** is the biggest lever for margin improvement

### Chart 04 — CM$ Ranking
- **Top 3 by total CM$**: Iced Coconut Latte ($140.8K), Drip Coffee ($82.4K), Latte Hot ($69.5K)
- These 3 items generate ~28% of all contribution margin
- **Bottom 3**: Pistachio Oat Latte Hot (-$149), Kyoto Matcha Smoothie ($717), Blood Orange CB ($1,522)
- The bottom 10 items collectively contribute <$30K — negligible margin impact

### Chart 05 — COGS Unit Economics
- **Pistachio Oat Latte (Hot)** at $3.57 COGS per unit — highest in the menu, with -$0.09 CM/unit
- **Pistachio Matcha** variants ($3.37, $3.21 COGS) are also margin-negative or near-zero
- The matcha-coconut and cold brew variants have high COGS ($2.06-$2.80) with thin margins
- Core lattes and drip coffee have favorable COGS:CM ratios (low COGS, high CM)
